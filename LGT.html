    Brief introduction on Logistic Regression


    In machine learning, a classification problem is grouping the data into predefined classes. An event is predicted where the response is categorical in nature or in other words, predicts the class for a given set of data. This can be performed on both structured or unstructured data. Structured data simply means where the target is already defined and unstructured or unlabeled data means where the target is unknown. The focus of this article is on structured data.

    Some of the instances of classes are: for an insurance company to categorize an insurance claim as fraud or genuine; a product manufactured is defective or not defective; for a telecom provider whether the customer will churn (i.e. leave the company) or not churn; whether a patient is diagnosed with cancer or not and alike. This is a binary classification where there are two classes.

    When there are more than two classes such as having high, medium, or low risk is a form of multi-class classification. The iris dataset having classes of flowers as Setosa, Versicolor, Virginica is an example of multi-class classification. In this article, will be working through a problem to predict whether the person is likely to have heart disease or not.
